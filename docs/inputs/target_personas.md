# Target Personas

## Primary Persona: The Technical PMM / Founding Marketer
**Title:** Product Marketing Manager, Head of Marketing, VP Marketing, or Founding Marketer at a developer tools company
**Company Stage:** Seed to Series B. Small marketing team (1 to 5 people). Often the marketer is the only marketer.
**Industry:** Developer tools, DevOps, AI/ML infrastructure, API companies, open-source companies
**Pain:** Responsible for launches, content, positioning, and sales enablement but drowning in execution. Generic AI writing tools produce content that sounds like consumer SaaS marketing. Developers ignore it. Every launch requires recreating the same assets from scratch with no institutional memory of what worked.
**Where They Spend Time Online:** Twitter/X (following dev tool founders and PMMs), LinkedIn (professional network), Hacker News (lurking), Product Hunt (launching), Lenny's Newsletter, First Round Review, developer marketing Slack communities
**What They Search For:** "developer marketing playbook," "how to launch a developer tool," "developer content strategy," "AI marketing tools for B2B," "product marketing for engineers"
**What Triggers Them to Evaluate:** A launch is coming up and they need to produce 10+ assets in 2 weeks. Or they just joined a dev tools company and need to build the marketing function from scratch.

## Secondary Persona: The Developer Advocate / DevRel
**Title:** Developer Advocate, Developer Relations Engineer, Developer Experience Engineer
**Company Stage:** Series A to Series C. DevRel team of 1 to 3 people.
**Industry:** Same as above
**Pain:** Writes technical content (blog posts, tutorials, docs) but gets pulled into launch support, social media, and sales enablement. Knows the product deeply but does not have marketing frameworks for positioning, messaging, or campaign planning. Spends time on repetitive content adaptation (turning a blog post into tweets, LinkedIn posts, email sequences).
**Where They Spend Time Online:** Twitter/X (primary), GitHub, Dev.to, YouTube, Discord servers for dev tools, Hacker News
**What They Search For:** "developer content automation," "AI for technical writing," "Claude Code projects," "content repurposing tools"
**What Triggers Them to Evaluate:** They see a Claude Code project that could save them hours of repetitive content work. Or they are preparing for a conference and need to generate follow-up sequences, social posts, and blog content simultaneously.

## Anti-Persona: Who This Is NOT For
- Enterprise marketing teams with 20+ people and established toolchains (Marketo, HubSpot, Salesforce Marketing Cloud). They need team workflows and approval chains, not a terminal tool.
- Consumer brand marketers. The content guidelines and voice are calibrated for technical audiences. Consumer copy requires a fundamentally different approach.
- Marketers who do not use the terminal. This requires Claude Code CLI. If someone lives entirely in browser-based tools, the friction of a terminal workflow is too high.
- Companies selling to non-technical buyers. The content guidelines assume the reader is an engineer, CTO, or technical leader.
