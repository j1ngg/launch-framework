# Product Hunt Launch Playbook for Developer Tools

This playbook covers the mechanics, timing, promotion, and post-launch execution for launching a developer tool on Product Hunt. It synthesizes tactics from top 2025/2026 launches including Cursor (5 launches, multiple #1 Day), Appwrite Sites (#1 Month), Kilo Code (#1 Day), Permit.io (#1 Day), Corbado (#1 Dev Tool of Week), and Stackfix (#1 Day and #1 Week with $0 paid spend).

Last updated: April 2026.

---

## The Algorithm: What Actually Determines Ranking

Product Hunt's algorithm changed dramatically in 2024 and 2025. Understanding it is prerequisite to every tactic in this playbook.

### Featured Status Is the Gatekeeper

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Percentage of launches that get featured | ~10% (down from 60 to 98% pre-2024) |
| Impact of featured status on success | ~70% |
| Traffic for non-featured products | 70% less than featured, regardless of upvote count |
| Who decides featuring | PH team, manually, based on Useful / Novel / High Craft / Creative criteria |

Developer tools face extra scrutiny. The PH CEO has stated they cannot feature every AI wrapper. Differentiation and polish in your submission assets (video, screenshots, copy) are evaluated before the community sees your product.

### Ranking Factors

| Factor | Weight | What It Means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Account age (365+ days, active) | ~10x vs new accounts | Veteran PH supporters matter 10x more than new signups |
| Comment quality | 1 quality comment ≈ 40 to 50 upvotes | 50 upvotes + 30 genuine comments beats 200 upvotes + 5 comments |
| First hour upvotes | 4x weighting vs later hours | Pre-launch prep quality shows up here |
| Vote velocity | Steady beats spiky | Spread outreach across 24 hours, not one burst |
| Geographic diversity | Flag trigger if absent | Concentrated voting from one region triggers review |
| First 4 hours | Counts hidden, random rotation | PH uses this window to detect artificial inflation |

### Upvote Quality Hierarchy

| Account Type | Approximate Weight |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 365+ day active accounts | ~10x |
| 3 to 6 month accounts with activity | 1x (baseline) |
| Accounts under 30 days | 0.1x or penalized |

Automatic vote clearing runs every ~2 hours, removing votes from new accounts, low engagement profiles, same IP clusters, and coordinated spikes.

**Safe upvote velocity: under 100 per hour.**

## Timing

The Product Hunt day runs 12:01 AM to 11:59 PM Pacific Time.

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| Best launch time | 12:01 AM PT (maximizes full 24 hour exposure) |
| Each hour earlier | ~8.7% more upvotes |
| Best day (high traffic) | Wednesday > Tuesday > Thursday |
| Best day (less competition) | Friday through Sunday (fewer votes needed, fewer visitors) |
| Developer tools specifically | Weekends can work due to higher developer browsing activity |
| Monthly badge potential | Target 2nd or 3rd week of month, on Tuesday or Wednesday |

### Geographic Wave Strategy for Launch Day

| Time (PT) | Wave | Who to Activate |
|-----------|------|----------------|
| 12:01 to 4 AM | Early bird | Core team and night owl supporters |
| 4 to 10 AM | European | London, Berlin, Paris contacts |
| 10 AM to 4 PM | US peak | Main email blast, social amplification (critical window) |
| 4 to 10 PM | Final push | West Coast follow ups, evening social posts |

If you are averaging fewer than 30 upvotes per hour during 6 to 9 AM PT, your pre-launch prep was insufficient.

## Preparation Timeline

### 6 Weeks Out

- Start participating in Product Hunt discussions (upvoting, commenting on other launches)
- Build follower base on the platform
- Monitor [hunted.space](https://hunted.space) for 2 to 4 weeks to establish realistic vote targets for your category
- Begin hunter outreach (see Hunter Strategy section)

### 4 Weeks Out

- Finalize product page
- Begin pre-launch outreach to warm contacts
- Identify 300 to 500 engaged supporters from established accounts (6+ months old)
- Send first email to subscribers explaining PH and asking them to create accounts

**Email template (account creation):**

> Subject: Quick favor before our Product Hunt launch
>
> We are launching [product] on Product Hunt on [date].
>
> If you do not have a Product Hunt account, creating one now and engaging with a few products over the next few weeks will make your support count significantly more on launch day. The algorithm heavily weights account age and activity.
>
> Here is a direct link to sign up: [link]
>
> Over the next few weeks, try upvoting and commenting on one or two products that interest you. That is all it takes.

### 3 Weeks Out

- Begin warm outreach to Tier 1 supporters (PH veterans) via LinkedIn DMs
- Prepare visual assets (see Visual Asset Spec section)
- Draft tagline options (test three versions with beta users)

**Outreach template (Tier 1 warm contacts):**

> Hey [name], we are launching [product] on Product Hunt on [date]. I would love your honest feedback on launch day. Would you be open to checking it out and leaving a comment?

### 2 Weeks Out

- Follow up email asking subscribers to upvote and comment on 2 to 3 products (account warming)
- Write maker's first comment (see Listing Anatomy section)
- Prepare launch kit for supporters (graphics, pre-written social posts, key talking points)
- Set up outreach tracking spreadsheet: contact name, channel, commitment status, follow up status

**Email template (account warming):**

> Subject: One small thing before [date]
>
> Could you spend 2 minutes on Product Hunt today? Upvote and comment on something that interests you. This helps your account carry more weight when we launch.
>
> Here are a few interesting products launching this week: [links]

### 1 Week Out

- Set PH teaser page live
- Ask Tier 1 supporters to click "Notify Me"
- Finalize all visual assets
- Brief supporters with launch kit

**Outreach template (Notify Me request):**

> We just published our Product Hunt launch page. Would you hit "Notify Me" so you get the alert on launch day? [link]

### 5 Days Out

- Personal outreach to top 20 contacts (phone call, text, voice message)
- Final review of all listing assets
- Confirm launch day team roles and timezone coverage

### 48 Hours Out

- Final review of maker's first comment
- Test all links in the listing
- Pre-draft social media posts for launch day (see Social Amplification section)
- Pre-draft email waves (see Email Promotion section)

## Hunter Strategy

Top-500 hunters get 3.2x more upvotes on average. Ben Lang (@benln, 50K+ followers) hunted both Cursor and Appwrite Sites in 2025.

### How to Find and Approach a Hunter

**Identify candidates:**
- Browse [Product Hunt leaderboard](https://www.producthunt.com/leaderboard) for active hunters in the developer tools category
- Look for hunters who have a development background or a track record of launching dev tools
- Credibility and relevant network matter more than raw follower count
- **Flo Merian ([x.com/fmerian](https://x.com/fmerian))** is a top resource for Product Hunt launches. He co-manages the Developer Marketing Community on Slack, hunted Corbado to #1 Developer Tool of the Week, and publishes detailed PH launch analyses (Appwrite, Cursor). Reach out to him early for hunter availability or PH optimization advice.

**Outreach timeline:**
- 6 weeks out: Identify 5 to 10 candidate hunters
- 5 weeks out: Engage with their content (comment on their posts, support products they hunt)
- 4 weeks out: Send a warm, concise DM explaining your product and why it fits their portfolio

**Hunter outreach template:**

> Hi [name], I have been following your hunts in the developer tools space, [reference a specific hunt they did]. We are building [one sentence product description] and planning to launch on Product Hunt around [target week]. Would you be interested in hunting it? Happy to give you early access so you can evaluate whether it is a fit.

**What to provide the hunter:**
- Early product access
- One paragraph product summary
- Draft tagline and description
- Visual assets
- Launch date and timezone plan

**Self-hunting is also viable.** Product Hunt states there is "no discernible advantage" to using a third-party hunter. Self-hunting gives you full control over timing and messaging. If you cannot secure a top hunter, self-hunt rather than settling for a low-quality one.

## Listing Anatomy

### Tagline (60 Characters Max)

The tagline appears on the Product Hunt homepage. It must answer "What is this?" in one sentence.

**Rules for developer tools:**
- Feature-first beats benefit-first. Developers parse feature statements faster than value propositions.
- The "[open-source] [known brand] alternative" formula works. Appwrite used "The open-source Vercel alternative." Chronicle used "Cursor for Slides."
- Test three versions with five people who have never seen the product. If they cannot repeat back what it does, rewrite it.

| Good taglines | Why they work |
|--------------|---------------|
| "Beautiful screen recordings with instant shareable links" | Concrete feature + concrete benefit |
| "The open-source Vercel alternative" | Known brand comparison, open-source positioning |
| "Cursor for Slides" | Instant category recognition |
| "Schedule Slack messages in advance" | Describes exactly what it does |

| Bad taglines | Why they fail |
|-------------|--------------|
| "The communication tool for async teams" | Vague. Could be anything. |
| "AI-powered developer productivity" | Buzzwords. Says nothing specific. |
| "The future of [category]" | Empty claim. |

### Description

- Lead with the problem, not the solution
- Structure: Problem statement → What you built → How it works
- 2 to 3 paragraphs maximum
- Opening hook example: "Your team spends 30 minutes daily re-explaining decisions in Slack. We changed that."

### Maker's First Comment (Under 800 Characters)

This comment signals quality to the algorithm. Write it 48 hours before launch.

**Structure:**
1. The problem and personal motivation (why you built this)
2. What changed or what is new (what makes this launch worth attention)
3. One concrete use case developers recognize
4. One clear CTA with zero signup friction (link to demo, playground, or sandbox)

**Rules:**
- No hype language
- No roadmap promises
- Anchor in user demand or existing adoption metrics if available
- Personal origin story of the problem resonates with developer audiences

**Example structure:**

> We built [product] because [specific problem we experienced firsthand]. After [X months / talking to Y developers / hitting Z limitation], we shipped [specific thing].
>
> [One concrete use case: "If you are doing X today, you can now Y in Z seconds."]
>
> Try it here (no signup required): [link to playground/demo]
>
> Would love to hear what you think, especially about [specific area for feedback].

### Visual Assets

| Asset | Spec | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Thumbnail | 240x240 px | Product logo or icon. Less than a third of top products use animated GIF thumbnails. |
| Gallery images | 1270x760 px, minimum 6 images (10+ recommended) | Show actual product workflows, not marketing graphics |
| Video | 60 to 90 seconds, 60fps, auto-plays muted | Authentic product use showing full workflow from start to finish |

**Gallery image sequence:**

| Slot | What to Show |
|------|-------------|
| Image 1 | Hero shot: main interface or biggest capability |
| Image 2 | The problem, and your product solving it (before/after) |
| Image 3 | Top feature with clean mockup |
| Image 4 | Second feature with clean mockup |
| Image 5 | Output/results screenshot (not just configuration) |
| Image 6+ | Additional workflows, integrations, or use cases |

**Rules:**
- Product screenshots only. No stock images. No marketing graphics. Cursor used 2 to 4 bare product screenshots per launch.
- All images must be mobile-readable
- A 30 second GIF demo at 60fps with upbeat audio yields 2x watch rate
- Video first 5 seconds are critical (auto-plays muted, must hook visually)
- Skip cinematic production. Authenticity performs better than polish.

## Promotion Strategy

### The Rules

Product Hunt explicitly bans asking for upvotes. "People should upvote things they genuinely like or find interesting, not because they were peer pressured to do so."

**What you CAN do:** Share your launch, bring people into the discussion, ask for honest feedback and comments.

**What gets you penalized:** Directly asking for upvotes, incentivizing upvotes, using bots, coordinated campaigns from new accounts, buying upvotes from services.

### Supporter Tiers

| Tier | Who | Why They Matter | How to Activate |
|------|-----|----------------|----------------|
| Tier 1: PH veterans | Accounts 6+ months old with engagement history | Carry ~10x vote weight | Personal DM on LinkedIn or Telegram, one at a time |
| Tier 2: Existing users/community | Email subscribers, Slack/Discord members, beta users | Already believe in the product | Email sequence starting 3 to 4 weeks out |
| Tier 3: Personal network | Friends, colleagues, investors, advisors | High willingness, low PH account quality | Ask to create accounts 2+ weeks early, engage daily to warm |

**Target: 300 to 500 committed supporters minimum.** Not followers. Not email subscribers. People who have committed to showing up on launch day.

### The Framing That Works

Since you cannot ask for upvotes, use one of these frames in all outreach:

| Frame | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Feedback frame | "We just launched. Would love your honest feedback." |
| Milestone frame | "After 6 months of building, we are finally live. Here is what we built and why." |
| Transparency frame | "We are live on Product Hunt. Here is our real time ranking. Every bit of support counts today." |

### Launch Day Outreach Templates

**DM to committed supporters (people who said yes during pre-launch):**

> Hi [name]. We launched [product] on Product Hunt today. As you kindly offered, would you support us on PH today? (It must be today to count.) [link]

Key: this references their prior commitment. Cold versions of this message perform terribly.

**DM to warm contacts (people who know you but have not committed):**

> Hey [name], we just launched [product] on Product Hunt. It [one sentence description of what it does]. Would love your honest take: [link]

### Email Promotion

Do not blast 100% of your list at midnight. This creates a velocity spike then collapse, which the algorithm reads as manipulation.

**Wave structure:**

| Wave | Time (PT) | Audience | % of List |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Wave 1 | 1:00 AM | Most engaged subscribers, timezone-appropriate contacts | 15 to 20% |
| Wave 2 | 8:00 AM | Main blast (US East Coast awake) | 40 to 50% |
| Wave 3 | 4:00 PM | Plain text follow up to non-openers | Remaining |

**Email template (main blast):**

> Subject: [Product] is live on Product Hunt
>
> After [X months] of building, [product] is live on Product Hunt today.
>
> [One sentence: the problem it solves.]
>
> [One sentence: what makes it different from the alternatives.]
>
> We would love your honest feedback. Check it out here: [Product Hunt link]
>
> If you have 30 seconds, leaving a comment about [specific aspect] helps more than anything else. The PH algorithm weights genuine comments heavily.
>
> [Founder name]

**Traffic source targets for day one:**
- 40% from your email list
- 30% from social media and communities
- 20% from personal network
- 10% organic Product Hunt traffic

### Social Amplification

**LinkedIn is the primary social channel for developer tool launches.**

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Account | Founder personal account, not brand page. Always. |
| Link placement | NEVER put the PH link in the post body. LinkedIn suppresses external links 30 to 50%. Put it in the first comment. |
| Post format | Personal story format, not announcement format |
| Number of posts | 3 to 5 across launch day |

**LinkedIn post structure (main announcement, 6 to 8 AM PT):**
1. Personal hook (1 to 2 sentences about the problem)
2. What you built (1 sentence)
3. Launch announcement (1 sentence)
4. Who it is for (1 sentence)
5. "Link in comments"

**Throughout the day:**
- 9 AM to 12 PM: Engage with every comment on your post
- 12 PM to 6 PM: Mid-day milestone update (ranking position, upvote count)
- Evening: Final push 2 to 3 hours before voting closes

**Twitter/X:**
- Announce on X first, build momentum, then launch PH (Cursor's pattern)
- Post stat updates and user testimonials throughout the 24 hour window
- Consider a Twitter Space with a dev influencer on launch day (Permit.io's tactic)

### Launch Kit for Supporters

Prepare and distribute to all committed supporters before launch day:

- 2 to 3 pre-written social posts (one for LinkedIn, one for Twitter, one generic)
- Product screenshots formatted for social sharing
- Key talking points (3 bullet summary of what the product does)
- The Product Hunt link
- One sentence they can copy-paste for comments

This makes amplification take 2 minutes instead of 20.

### Community Activation

| Community | How to Use |
|-----------|-----------|
| Your own Slack/Discord | Drop the link for early velocity. These people already trust you. |
| Indie Hackers | Post your launch story (not a link drop). Lead with lessons learned. |
| r/SaaS, r/SideProject | Contribute value for weeks before launch. Story format on launch day. |
| Adjacent open source project communities | "This tool solves a problem your community has" framing. Not upvote requests. |
| Dev tool Slack groups | Active participation required weeks before you can credibly share |

**The test:** Would you share this in this community even if you were not launching today? If no, it is spam.

### Cross-Promotion Partnerships

Set up mutual launch agreements with 5 to 10 companies with complementary (not competing) products.

**How to set up:**
1. Support their launches first (genuine comments, upvotes from real accounts)
2. After supporting them, reach out: "I noticed you launched [product] recently. We are planning our launch for [date]. Would you be open to a mutual support arrangement?"
3. Share launch kits with each other
4. On launch day, each company shares the other's launch through their channels

This consistently generates higher quality engagement than any other partnership tactic.

### What NOT to Do

| Tactic | What Happens |
|--------|-------------|
| Buying upvotes (Fiverr, Upvote.club) | PH detects and removes. Products have lost #1 Day after penalty. |
| Sending 100% of traffic at 12:01 AM | Velocity spike then collapse. Algorithm reads as manipulation. |
| Creating new accounts to vote | 0.1x weight. Mass new accounts trigger flags. |
| Multiple votes from same IP | Immediate spam filter, vote removal, potential shadow ban. |
| Generic comments ("Great product!") | Near zero algorithm weight. |
| Cold outreach to strangers | Low conversion, triggers coordinated campaign detection. |
| Paid Twitter KOLs under $5K budget | $2K on 10 posts fades in 48 hours. Same budget on LinkedIn DMs generates 300 to 500 quality upvotes. |

## Post-Launch Execution

### The 72 Hour Cliff

Expect 80 to 90% traffic drop within three days. Most Product Hunt launches do not fail on Product Hunt. They fail after Product Hunt because founders do not convert the spike.

### Day by Day Playbook

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 to 2 | Send welcome emails directing PH visitors to the "aha moment." Reduce friction (SSO, simple flows). Create dedicated onboarding path. |
| Day 1 | Thank-you post and launch recap on social. Respond to every remaining PH comment. |
| Day 3 | Publish tutorial or quick-start guide. Extract emails from engaged PH commenters. |
| Day 7 | Case study or early win story. Follow up with journalists who covered the launch. |
| Day 14 | Early access offer or discount for PH community. |
| Day 3 to 30 | Self-serve track: onboarding, upgrade prompt, pricing clarity. Sales-assisted track: lead capture, fast demo loop, pilot offers. |

### Conversion Benchmarks (Featured Products)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Visitors | 1,000 to 5,000 |
| Visitor to signup | 15 to 25% |
| Signup to activated (7 days) | 35 to 45% |
| Activated to paying (30 days) | 5 to 10% |
| Signups | 10 to 150 |

Non-featured products: 100 to 500 visitors, 1 to 15 signups.

### Badge Placement

Deploy PH badges on:
- GitHub README (hero section)
- Landing page
- Email signatures
- Pitch decks

Badges provide ongoing social proof well beyond launch day.

## Multi-Launch Strategy

Treat Product Hunt as a recurring channel, not a one-time event. Each major feature update is a legitimate new launch that compounds social proof and followers.

**Reference points:**
- Cursor: 5 launches in 2025, each major feature was a separate launch
- Supabase: 16 total launches, only 9 received awards, but cumulative momentum made them a household name
- Kilo Code: 2 launches, second one (JetBrains support) outperformed the first

**What qualifies as a new launch:**
- Major feature release that changes what the product can do
- New platform or integration (e.g., mobile, JetBrains, VS Code)
- Significant version milestone (2.0, 3.0)

**What does NOT qualify:**
- Bug fixes or minor improvements
- UI refreshes without functional changes
- Feature updates that only matter to existing users

**Compounding mechanics:**
- Each launch adds PH followers who get notified of your next launch
- Badges accumulate (Product of Day, Week, Month)
- Weekly badge = 7x daily exposure, Monthly badge = 30x daily exposure

## Budget Reality

| Company | Spend | Result |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Stackfix | $0 paid (warm outreach only) | #1 Day, #1 Week, 1,200+ upvotes |
| Permit.io | $800 (Twitter Space, Instagram, YouTube) | #1 Day, 500% signup spike |
| Corbado | Organic focus | #1 Dev Tool of Week, #4 Day |

The highest performing developer tool launches spend near zero on paid promotion and invest everything in pre-launch relationship building and launch day coordination.

The consensus from practitioners: under $5K budget, skip paid channels entirely. Invest in a dedicated LinkedIn DM operation. Three people running outreach for one week generates 300 to 500 quality upvotes from real users with aged accounts.
